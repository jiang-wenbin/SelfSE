SelfSE: Self-Supervised Speech Enhancement without Clean Speech Online Supplement

Authors

Wenbin Jiang, Yifan Zhang, Fei Wen, Kai Yu

Abstract

Most deep neural network (DNN) based speech enhancement methods usually train the model in a supervised manner, which requires massive noisy-to-clean data pairs for training. However, due to the complexity and diversity of realistic environments, it is difﬁcult or even impractical to collect the ground-truth clean speech in some applications. To address this problem, we propose a novel self-supervised learning method for speech enhancement that does not require clean reference speech. Specifically, we first use the unprocessed noisy speech and noise to construct noisier-to-noisy data pairs in the first training epoch. Then, in the subsequent training epochs, we iteratively construct noisier-to-noisy data pairs based on the model learned in the previous epoch to train a better model. Experimental results on the popular benchmark dataset show that the proposed method achieves competitive performance with the state-of-the-art supervised learning method.
